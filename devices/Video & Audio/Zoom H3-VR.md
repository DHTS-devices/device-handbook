# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  Zoom H3-VR

- Device type:
  360° Audio Recorder / Ambisonics Recorder / VR Audio Recorder

- Category:
  Video & Audio

- Manufacturer:
  Zoom

- Overview:
  The Zoom H3-VR is a portable recorder designed specifically for recording spatial audio and 360-degree sound environments. Unlike a normal stereo recorder such as the Zoom H1n, the H3-VR records sound from multiple directions at the same time using a built-in Ambisonics microphone array.

  The recorder contains four matched microphone capsules arranged to capture full spatial audio around the recorder instead of only left and right stereo sound. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

  The H3-VR is mainly used for:
  - 360 video production
  - VR recording
  - environmental sound capture
  - immersive audio
  - spatial audio experiments
  - ambience recording
  - sound effects recording
  - surround audio production

  One of the biggest differences between the H3-VR and traditional Ambisonics setups is that the H3-VR performs Ambisonics encoding internally. Normally, VR audio workflows require:
  - a separate Ambisonics microphone
  - an external recorder
  - computer encoding afterward

  The H3-VR combines these into one device. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

  The recorder supports:
  - Ambisonics recording
  - binaural recording
  - stereo recording
  - USB audio interface mode

  The device is often mounted:
  - under 360 cameras
  - on tripods
  - in room centers
  - in environmental recording locations

- Primary use cases:
  - 360 video audio

  - Spatial ambience recording

  - Immersive audio experiments

- Best for:

- Binaural recording
- Stereo recording
- Spatial audio capture
- Motion sensor alignment
- VR audio workflows
- USB microphone mode
- Real-time binaural monitoring
- Audio rotation during playback

- Feature notes:
  The biggest feature is onboard Ambisonics conversion.

  Traditional VR audio workflows often require converting Ambisonics A-format into B-format later on a computer.

  The H3-VR performs this internally, simplifying the workflow significantly. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

- Important practical note:
  This recorder is designed mainly for environmental and spatial recording.

  It is NOT ideal for:
  - handheld interviews
  - close-up dialogue recording
  - isolated speech recording

  because the microphones capture sound from all directions.

# 11. Troubleshooting

## Excessive Handling Noise

Possible causes:
- Recorder touched during recording
- Recorder mounted loosely
- Tripod vibration

Possible solutions:
- Use stable tripod
- Avoid touching recorder
- Isolate from vibration

- Notes:
  Spatial microphones capture handling noise much more aggressively than standard stereo microphones.

---

## Wind Noise

Possible causes:
- Outdoor recording
- Air movement around microphones

Possible solutions:
- Use furry windscreen
- Reduce direct wind exposure
- Move recorder to protected location

---

## Audio Orientation Sounds Incorrect

Possible causes:
- Wrong Ambisonics orientation
- Recorder mounted upside down
- Incorrect export settings

Possible solutions:
- Re-orient audio during editing
- Verify motion sensor settings
- Check Ambisonics export configuration

---

## Distorted Audio

Possible causes:
- Extremely loud environment
- Incorrect gain settings
- Wind overload

Possible solutions:
- Lower gain
- Use wind protection
- Reposition recorder

---

## File Compatibility Problems

Possible causes:
- Software lacks Ambisonics support
- Incorrect channel interpretation

Possible solutions:
- Use Zoom Ambisonics Player
- Convert to stereo or binaural
- Use supported editing software

- Compared to normal handheld recorders:

  - supports Ambisonics workflows
  - designed for VR and 360 content

- The recorder works best when:
  - mounted away from the operator
  - used in immersive environments
  - paired with 360 cameras
  - recording ambience rather than isolated speech

  - an environmental sound capture device
  - a spatial audio recorder
  - a VR production tool

  rather than a normal interview recorder.

- Common beginner mistake:
  Holding the recorder in the hand during recording.

- The recorder is especially useful for:
  - environmental ambience
