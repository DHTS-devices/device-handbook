# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  Kandao QooCam 8K

- Device type:
  360° Camera / 8K VR Camera / Immersive Video Camera

- Category:
  Video & Audio

- Manufacturer:
  KanDao

- Overview:
  The Kandao QooCam 8K is a professional-grade portable 360 camera designed for:
  - immersive video
  - VR content
  - 360 filmmaking
  - virtual tours
  - panoramic photography
  - spatial storytelling
  - social media reframing workflows

  The camera became widely known because it was one of the first relatively compact consumer-level 360 cameras capable of recording true 8K 360 video. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

  Unlike traditional cameras that frame only one direction, the QooCam 8K captures the full surrounding environment simultaneously using:
  - dual fisheye lenses
  - dual large 1/1.7-inch sensors
  - 360 stitching workflow

  The camera is commonly used for:
  - VR filmmaking
  - immersive documentaries
  - YouTube 360 videos
  - virtual tourism
  - event capture
  - real estate walkthroughs
  - social media reframing
  - cinematic 360 production

  One of the biggest strengths of the QooCam 8K compared to many older 360 cameras is image quality. The larger sensors and 10-bit recording provide:
  - better low-light performance
  - improved dynamic range
  - cleaner shadows
  - smoother gradients
  - sharper VR playback

  compared to many smaller-sensor consumer 360 cameras. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

  The camera is especially useful because footage can later be:
  - viewed in VR headsets
  - exported as normal widescreen video
  - reframed into standard social media content
  - stabilized digitally

# 7.4 Understanding 360 Video

The camera records:
```text
FULL SPHERE VIDEO
```

This means:
- front
- back
- left
- right
- up
- down

are all recorded simultaneously.

Unlike normal cameras:
you do NOT choose framing during recording.

You choose framing later during editing.

---

# 7.5 Reframing Workflow

One of the most important workflows.

Typical process:
1. Shoot full 360 video
2. Import into QooCam Studio
3. Choose viewing angle later
4. Add digital camera movement
5. Export widescreen video

This allows:
- fake camera pans
- digital tracking
- social media reframing
- cinematic edits

from one recording.

# 7.7 Stitching Workflow

360 cameras record separate fisheye images that must be stitched together.

Typical workflow:
1. Import footage
2. Run stitching
3. Adjust horizon
4. Correct stitch lines
5. Export final video

Common stitching issues:
- close objects crossing stitch line
- hands near camera
- subjects too close
- lighting mismatch

---

# 11. Troubleshooting

## Stitching Artifacts

Possible causes:
- subject too close
- crossing stitch line
- lighting mismatch

Possible solutions:
- increase subject distance
- reposition camera
- adjust stitch manually

---

## Overheating

Possible causes:
- long 8K recording
- hot weather
- insufficient airflow

Possible solutions:
- shorten recording sessions
- cool camera
- avoid direct sunlight

- Notes:
  The camera contains an internal cooling fan. Some users report audible fan noise in quiet environments. :contentReference[oaicite:25]{index=25}

---

## Large File Problems

Possible causes:
- 8K bitrate extremely high
- slow storage
- insufficient SSD space

Possible solutions:
- use U3 microSD cards
- use SSD editing drive
- archive projects regularly

---

## Wi-Fi Connection Problems

Possible causes:
- interference
- outdated firmware
- phone compatibility

Possible solutions:
- update firmware
- reconnect Wi-Fi
- restart camera and app

---

## VR Playback Looks Soft

Possible causes:
- headset limitations
- low export bitrate
- unrealistic VR expectations

Possible solutions:
- export highest bitrate
- sharpen carefully
- use high-resolution headset
