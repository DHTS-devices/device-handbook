# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  Zoom F6

- Device type:
  Multi-Track Field Recorder / Location Sound Recorder

- Category:
  Video & Audio

- Manufacturer:
  Zoom

- Overview:
  The Zoom F6 is a professional multi-track field recorder designed for film production, interviews, documentaries, field recording, and location sound work.

  Unlike small portable recorders such as the Zoom F2-BT, the F6 is designed for multi-microphone recording workflows. It supports up to six separate microphone inputs simultaneously and can record multiple isolated tracks at the same time.

  The F6 became well known because it was one of the first compact field recorders to support 32-bit float recording together with dual A/D converters. This allows the recorder to capture both very loud and very quiet sounds with less risk of clipped audio. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

  The recorder is commonly used for:
  - documentary production
  - filmmaking
  - interview recording
  - boom microphone recording
  - location sound
  - field research
  - environmental recording
  - backup audio during video production

  Compared to small handheld recorders, the F6 is designed more like a professional production recorder:
  - multiple XLR microphone inputs
  - timecode support
  - external power options
  - professional preamps
  - isolated track recording
  - field bag workflows

  The device is especially useful when recording:
  - multiple people
  - boom + lavalier combinations
  - unpredictable environments
  - difficult sound situations
  - one-time events where clipping cannot be risked

- Primary use cases:

# 5. Main Features

- 32-bit float recording
- Dual A/D converters
- Six isolated XLR inputs
- Timecode support
- Multi-track recording
- Professional microphone preamps
- AutoMix
- USB audio interface support
- Multiple power options
- Field production workflow support

- Feature notes:
  The biggest feature is 32-bit float recording. In traditional recording workflows, the sound recordist must carefully adjust gain during recording to avoid clipping.

  With the F6, audio levels are much more forgiving because of the recorder’s dual A/D converter design and 32-bit float workflow. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

  This is especially useful in:
  - documentaries
  - interviews
  - fieldwork
  - unpredictable environments
  - solo operator workflows

  where sudden loud sounds may happen unexpectedly.

- Important practical note:
  32-bit float does NOT mean:
  - microphones cannot distort
  - bad mic placement is acceptable
  - clothing noise disappears
  - wind noise is fixed automatically

  The microphone itself can still overload physically even if the recorder file does not clip. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

---

## 7.2 Setup Before Recording

1. Insert formatted SD card
2. Connect power source
3. Connect microphones
4. Enable phantom power if needed
5. Put on monitoring headphones
6. Verify microphone signals
7. Check recording format
8. Verify timecode settings if used
9. Do a test recording
10. Listen to the test recording
11. Start official recording

---

## 7.3 Typical Recording Workflow

Example documentary workflow:

- Input 1:
  Boom microphone

- Input 2:
  Interview subject lavalier

- Input 3:
  Interviewer lavalier

- Input 4:
  Environmental backup microphone

Workflow:
1. Mount recorder in sound bag
2. Route microphones into inputs
3. Monitor levels with headphones
4. Record isolated tracks
5. Create stereo mix
6. Export files after recording
7. Sync audio with video during editing

---

## 7.4 Field Usage Notes

Before recording:
- Check battery levels
- Verify phantom power settings
- Confirm microphones are connected securely
- Monitor with headphones
- Verify SD card space

During recording:
- Watch track meters
- Monitor headphone output
- Listen for cable noise
- Listen for RF interference
- Watch for accidental muted channels

After recording:
- Rename files
- Back up recordings immediately
- Verify files open correctly
- Save original raw files

# 11. Troubleshooting

## No Audio on One Channel

Possible causes:
- XLR cable disconnected
- Phantom power disabled
- Wireless receiver battery dead
- Channel muted

Possible solutions:
- Check cable connection
- Enable phantom power
- Replace wireless battery
- Verify track arming

---

## Headphone Monitoring Problems

Possible causes:
- Headphone volume low
- Routing issue
- Wrong monitoring mode

Possible solutions:
- Increase headphone volume
- Verify routing settings
- Check monitoring source

---

## Distorted Audio

Possible causes:
- Microphone overload
- Wind hitting microphone
- Faulty cable
- Wireless interference

Possible solutions:
- Move microphone
- Use windscreen
- Replace cable
- Check wireless frequency

- Notes:
  Even with 32-bit float recording, microphones themselves can still physically distort. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

---

## Recorder Stops During Recording

Possible causes:
- SD card too slow
- Battery failure
- Corrupted card

Possible solutions:
- Use recommended SD cards
- Replace batteries
- Format card
- Use backup power source

---

## Timecode Drift

Possible causes:
- Incorrect settings
- Unsynced devices

Possible solutions:
- Rejam timecode
- Verify frame rate
- Check TC settings

# 12. Notes

- The Zoom F6 is designed for professional location sound workflows rather than casual voice memos or handheld recording.

- Compared to small recorders like the Zoom H1 or F2:
  - more inputs
  - professional XLR support
  - timecode
  - better production workflows
  - stronger power options
  - isolated track recording

- The recorder is especially useful when:
  - multiple microphones are needed
  - the environment is unpredictable
  - audio cannot be rerecorded
  - video synchronization is important

- Practical advice:
  32-bit float is a safety net, not magic.

  Good microphone placement, monitoring, and recording habits are still extremely important.

- Many professional users treat the F6 as:
  - a lightweight documentary recorder
  - a backup film recorder
  - a compact production sound recorder
  - a field interview recorder

  depending on the workflow and budget.
