  - VR still benefits heavily from higher resolutions
  - 8K VR can still appear softer than expected inside headsets

  compared to standard flat-screen 8K video.

# 7.4 VR Camera Placement Rules

This is one of the most important parts of VR production.

Good placement:
- center of action
- natural human viewing position
- stable support
- eye-level perspective

Bad placement:
- too close to walls
- operator standing near camera
- unstable ground
- cluttered stitching zones

- Important practical note:
  In 360 VR production:
  EVERYTHING around the camera is visible.

  This includes:
  - crew members
  - lights
  - cables
  - shadows
  - tripods
  - operators

  VR production requires thinking in full 360 degrees.

# 7.5 Stitching Workflow

The Pro 2 workflow usually includes stitching multiple camera feeds into one VR video.

Typical workflow:
1. Import lens footage
2. Sync footage
3. Stitch panorama
4. Adjust stitching lines
5. Export VR master
6. Edit final project

- Notes:
  Stitching errors become more visible when:
  - subjects are too close
  - fast movement crosses lens boundaries
  - lighting changes heavily
  - parallax becomes extreme

# 11. Troubleshooting

## Stitching Artifacts

Possible causes:
- subject too close
- rapid movement
- incorrect stitching alignment

Possible solutions:
- increase camera distance
- adjust stitch lines
- avoid close-range crossings

---

## Dropped Frames

Possible causes:
- slow memory cards
- overheating
- storage mismatch

Possible solutions:
- use certified V30 cards
- cool camera
- match all cards

---

## VR Footage Looks Soft

Possible causes:
- unrealistic VR expectations
- headset limitations
- low bitrate export

Possible solutions:
- increase export quality
- sharpen carefully
- use proper VR headset playback

---

## FarSight Connection Problems

Possible causes:
- interference
- antenna issue
- distance limitations

Possible solutions:
- reposition antennas
- reduce interference
- verify transmitter connection

---

## Overheating

Possible causes:
- long 8K recording
- hot environments
- insufficient airflow

Possible solutions:
- allow cooling periods
- avoid direct sunlight
- improve ventilation
