# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  REED Instruments Sound Level Meter

- Device type:
  Sound Level Meter / Noise Meter / Decibel Meter

- Category:
  Physiological Testing

- Manufacturer:
  REED Instruments

- Overview:
  The REED Instruments Sound Level Meter is a handheld device used to measure environmental sound levels and acoustic intensity in decibels (dB). The device is commonly used in workplace safety, environmental monitoring, laboratory testing, equipment noise analysis, and acoustic research.

  The meter measures sound pressure levels through a condenser microphone and displays readings in decibels (dB). Depending on the model, the device may support real-time monitoring, data logging, minimum/maximum recording, and frequency weighting modes.

- Primary use cases:
  - Noise level monitoring
  - Environmental sound measurement
  - Acoustic testing
  - Laboratory safety measurements
  - Equipment noise evaluation
  - Classroom or room acoustic analysis

- Best for:

# 7. Setup & Workflow

## 7.1 Requirements

Required:
- Batteries
- Sound level meter device

Optional:
- Tripod
- SD card
- PC software
- Sound calibrator

Recommended accessories:
- Windshield ball
- Carrying case
- Acoustic calibrator

---

## 7.2 Setup Steps

1. Insert batteries
2. Power on device
3. Select measurement range
4. Select A or C weighting
5. Select Fast or Slow response mode
6. Begin measurement

Optional:
7. Mount on tripod for long-term recording
8. Insert SD card if using logging model

---

## 7.3 Daily Usage Workflow

Typical workflow:
1. Power on device
2. Verify microphone is unobstructed
3. Select proper weighting mode
4. Position device at measurement location
5. Record measurements
6. Export or log data if required
7. Power off device after use

- Usage notes:
  Avoid handling or touching the microphone during measurements since this can introduce additional noise artifacts.
