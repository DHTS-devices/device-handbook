# Zoom VRH-8

---

# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  Zoom VRH-8

- Device type:
  Ambisonics Microphone Capsule / Spatial Audio Capsule

- Category:
  Video & Audio

- Manufacturer:
  Zoom

- Overview:
  The Zoom VRH-8 is an Ambisonics microphone capsule designed specifically for the Zoom H8 recorder. Instead of functioning as a standalone recorder, the VRH-8 attaches directly to the H8 through Zoom’s Capsule System 2.0 and turns the H8 into a portable spatial audio recorder. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

  The capsule contains four condenser microphones arranged for 360-degree spatial recording. Unlike normal stereo microphones that record left and right channels only, the VRH-8 captures audio from all directions at the same time. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

  The VRH-8 is mainly used for:
  - 360 video production
  - VR audio
  - immersive sound recording
  - environmental ambience recording
  - spatial audio experiments
  - surround sound workflows
  - AR and mixed reality content

  One important difference between the VRH-8 and older Ambisonics workflows is that the H8 + VRH-8 combination performs Ambisonics decoding internally. Normally, Ambisonics recording requires:
  - raw microphone recording
  - later A-format to B-format conversion
  - additional software processing

  The VRH-8 simplifies this process with onboard decoding support. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

  The VRH-8 is often compared to the Zoom H3-VR. In practice, the sound quality is very similar because the capsule system is closely related to the H3-VR microphone design. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

  Zoom Capsule System 2.0

# 5. Main Features

- 360-degree audio capture
- Ambisonics recording
- Built-in Ambisonics decoding
- Spatial audio workflows
- Four-microphone condenser array
- Automatic orientation alignment
- Compact portable design
- Integrated H8 workflow

- Feature notes:
  The biggest advantage of the VRH-8 is portability.

  Traditional Ambisonics systems usually require:
  - separate microphone arrays
  - external recorders
  - post-production conversion workflows

  The VRH-8 combines these into a small portable setup with the H8. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

- Important practical note:
  The VRH-8 is designed for environmental and immersive recording.

  It is NOT ideal for:
  - close-up interviews
  - isolated speech recording
  - handheld dialogue recording

  because the microphones capture sound from all directions simultaneously.

# 7. Setup & Workflow

## 7.1 Requirements

Required:
- Zoom H8 recorder
- SD card
- Power source for H8

Optional:
- Tripod
- 360 camera
- Windscreen
- Headphones
- External battery pack

Recommended:
- Stable tripod
- Large SD card
- Closed-back headphones
- Wind protection for outdoor recording

---

## 7.2 Physical Setup

1. Remove existing H8 microphone capsule
2. Align VRH-8 connector with H8 capsule slot
3. Slide capsule into H8 until locked
4. Power on H8
5. Verify VRH-8 detection
6. Select Ambisonics recording mode

- Notes:
  The capsule should never be forced into the connector.

  Always power off the H8 before attaching or removing the capsule.

---

## 7.3 Recording Setup

Before recording:
1. Verify SD card space
2. Check battery levels
3. Attach windscreen if outdoors
4. Mount recorder on tripod
5. Monitor recording with headphones
6. Do short test recording
7. Listen for handling noise or wind

- Important practical note:
  Handling noise becomes extremely noticeable in spatial audio recordings.

  Avoid:
  - touching the recorder
  - handheld operation
  - unstable mounting surfaces

---

## 7.4 Typical Recording Workflow

### 360 Video Workflow

1. Mount VRH-8 below 360 camera
2. Start H8 recording
3. Start camera recording
4. Leave recording area if possible
5. Record environmental audio
6. Export Ambisonics files
7. Sync audio with video during editing

---

### Environmental Ambience Workflow

1. Place recorder in center of recording area
2. Avoid nearby walls
3. Use tripod
4. Record room or outdoor ambience
5. Export Ambisonics files
6. Convert or monitor in binaural format

---

# 7.5 Placement Recommendations

Good placement:
- center of room
- open environments
- below 360 camera
- stable tripod

Bad placement:
- directly beside walls
- handheld recording
- noisy handling environments
- close to vibrating objects

- Notes:
  Spatial audio captures the entire environment.

  This means:
  - footsteps
  - breathing
  - operator movement
  - cable movement
  - wind

  become much more obvious than normal stereo recording.

# 11. Troubleshooting

## Capsule Not Detected

Possible causes:
- Improper connection
- Dirty connector
- Unsupported recorder

Possible solutions:
- Reattach capsule
- Clean connector carefully
- Verify using Zoom H8

- Notes:
  The VRH-8 is designed specifically for the H8. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

---

## Excessive Handling Noise

Possible causes:
- Recorder touched during recording
- Loose mounting
- Table vibration

Possible solutions:
- Use tripod
- Avoid touching recorder
- Isolate from vibration

---

## Wind Noise

Possible causes:
- Outdoor recording
- Air movement around microphones

Possible solutions:
- Use furry windscreen
- Move to protected area
- Reduce direct wind exposure

---

## Spatial Orientation Sounds Incorrect

Possible causes:
- Incorrect orientation
- Wrong export settings
- Ambisonics conversion issue

Possible solutions:
- Re-orient during editing
- Verify Ambisonics settings
- Check playback software

---

## File Compatibility Problems

Possible causes:
- Editing software lacks Ambisonics support
- Incorrect channel interpretation

Possible solutions:
- Use Ambisonics-compatible software
- Convert to stereo or binaural
- Verify export format

# 12. Notes

- The VRH-8 is best understood as:
  - a spatial audio capsule
  - an Ambisonics microphone system
  - a VR recording accessory

  rather than a normal microphone.

- Compared to standard stereo microphones:
  - captures sound from all directions
  - records immersive environments
  - supports spatial playback
  - designed for VR workflows

- Practical advice:
  Think carefully about the entire environment before recording.

  In spatial recording:
  - every movement
  - every sound source
  - every reflection

  becomes part of the immersive experience.

- Common beginner mistake:
  Standing too close to the recorder during recording.

  Because the microphones capture 360-degree sound, the operator often becomes unintentionally present in the recording.

- The VRH-8 works best for:
  - VR production
  - 360 video
  - immersive ambience
  - environmental soundscapes
  - surround audio experiments
  - spatial sound design
