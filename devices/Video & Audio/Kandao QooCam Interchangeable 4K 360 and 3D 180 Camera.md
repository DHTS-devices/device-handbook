# Kandao QooCam Interchangeable 4K 360 and 3D 180 Camera

---

# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  Kandao QooCam Interchangeable 4K 360 and 3D 180 Camera

- Device type:
  360° Camera / VR180 Camera / 3D Camera

- Category:
  Video & Audio

- Manufacturer:
  KanDao

- Overview:
  The Kandao QooCam is one of the first consumer cameras designed to switch between:
  - 360° video mode
  - stereoscopic 3D 180° VR mode

  using a physical folding lens design. 

  Unlike most 360 cameras that only capture monoscopic panoramic video, the QooCam can physically transform into:
  - a normal 360 camera
  - a VR180 stereoscopic camera

  by rotating the hinged body. 

  The camera uses:
  - three fisheye lenses
  - Sony CMOS sensors
  - IMU stabilization
  - depth mapping

  to capture immersive VR content. 

  One of the biggest reasons the QooCam became popular in early VR creator communities is because it allowed creators to shoot:
  - VR180 3D
  - 360 video
  - depth-mapped photos

  in one portable device without needing a large VR rig.

  The camera is commonly used for:
  - VR180 filmmaking
  - 360 social media
  - immersive travel video
  - stereoscopic VR
  - virtual tourism
  - immersive storytelling
  - experimental VR filmmaking

  The QooCam also supports:
  - depth mapping
  - refocus effects
  - stabilization
  - overcapture
  - timelapse
  - slow motion

  through the QooCam software ecosystem.

  - immersive VR180
  - panoramic 360
  - reframed flat video

  all in one system. :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}

- Important practical note:
  VR180 and 360 are very different shooting styles.

  360:
  - captures everything around camera

  VR180:
  - captures only front hemisphere
  - allows creator to stand behind camera

  Many creators prefer VR180 because:
  - easier storytelling
  - less stitching problems
  - easier to hide crew
  - stronger depth perception


Useful for:
- VR storytelling
- immersive depth
- easier editing

# 7.5 Basic 360 Workflow

1. Put camera in straight mode
2. Mount on tripod/selfie stick
3. Press record
4. Leave recording area
5. Record environment
6. Transfer footage
7. Stitch/export

Important:
Everything around the camera becomes visible.

---

# 7.6 Basic VR180 Workflow

1. Fold camera into VR180 mode
2. Point lenses toward scene
3. Stay behind camera
4. Record immersive scene
5. Export VR180 footage

Advantages of VR180:
- easier storytelling
- easier framing
- stronger depth effect
- less stitching artifacts

This simulates:

- focus pulling
- portrait-style blur

after shooting.

# 11. Troubleshooting

## Stitching Artifacts

Possible causes:
- subjects too close
- crossing stitch lines
- improper positioning

Possible solutions:
- increase subject distance
- reposition camera
- adjust stitching manually

---

## Wi-Fi Connection Problems

Possible causes:
- interference
- outdated firmware
- phone compatibility

Possible solutions:
- restart camera
- reconnect Wi-Fi
- update firmware

---

## VR180 Depth Looks Incorrect

Possible causes:
- subject too close
- improper VR spacing
- bad alignment

Possible solutions:
- increase subject distance
- stabilize camera
- avoid fast movement

---

## Blurry Footage

Possible causes:
- dirty lens
- low light
- stabilization overload

Possible solutions:
- clean lenses
- improve lighting
- stabilize movement

---

## Large File Problems

Possible causes:
- high bitrate recording
- slow storage

Possible solutions:
- use fast SD card
- transfer files regularly

  - 360 video

  - an immersive storytelling tool

  rather than a normal action camera.

- The QooCam is especially strong for:
