# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  Datacolor LightColor Meter

- Full name:
  Datacolor LightColor Meter (LCM200)

- Device type:
  Light Meter / Color Temperature Meter / Lighting Calibration Device

- Category:
  Imaging & Environmental Measurement

- Manufacturer:
  Datacolor

- Overview:
  The Datacolor LightColor Meter is a professional lighting measurement tool designed for:

  - photography lighting
  - videography
  - cinematography
  - XR studio setup
  - virtual production
  - color consistency
  - lighting calibration
  - environmental lighting measurement
  - research imaging environments

  Unlike basic light meters that only measure:

  ```text
  brightness
  ```

  the LightColor Meter measures:

  ```text
  LIGHT
  +
  COLOR TEMPERATURE
  +
  DUV (green/magenta tint)
  ```

  in real time. It connects wirelessly to a smartphone app and helps photographers, filmmakers, and researchers achieve:

  ```text
  CONSISTENT LIGHTING
  +
  ACCURATE WHITE BALANCE
  ```

  across scenes and devices. It works with:

  ```text
  daylight
  LED
  tungsten
  HMI
  fluorescent
  flash/strobe
  ```

  lighting systems. 

  Think of it as:

  ```text
  LIGHT SENSOR
  ↓
  COLOR ACCURACY
  ↓
  BETTER CAMERA RESULTS
  ```

# 6. What Does It Measure?

---

## Light Intensity

Measures:

```text
lux
EV
exposure
```

Useful for:

- studio photography
- video shoots
- consistent exposure
- XR room lighting

Example:

```text
too dark
↓
increase light
```

instead of guessing.

---

## Color Temperature (VERY IMPORTANT)

Measures:

```text
Kelvin (K)
```

Examples:

```text
3200K
warm tungsten

5600K
daylight
```

This matters because:

```text
different lights
often DO NOT MATCH
```

even if manufacturers claim:

```text
5600K
```

Useful for:

```text
matching LEDs
XR filming
green screen
medical recording
```. 

---

## DUV (Green/Magenta Shift)

Very important for professional imaging.

Measures:

```text
green shift
or
magenta shift
```

Useful for:

```text
LED matching
film production
camera consistency
```

Example:

```text
two LEDs
same Kelvin
BUT
different tint
```

DUV helps identify this.

---

## Flash / Strobe

Can measure:

```text

```

Useful for:

```text
