# Varjo XR-3

---

# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  Varjo XR-3

- Device type:
  Professional Mixed Reality Headset

- Category:
  XR / VR / AR

- Manufacturer:
  Varjo

- Overview:
  The Varjo XR-3 is a high-end mixed reality headset designed for:
  - simulation
  - training
  - research
  - industrial visualization
  - medical XR
  - engineering review
  - enterprise XR development

  Unlike standalone headsets such as Quest or VIVE XR Elite, the XR-3 is mainly a PC-powered professional XR system.

  It combines:
  - ultra high-resolution displays
  - video passthrough mixed reality
  - LiDAR depth sensing
  - eye tracking
  - hand tracking
  - SteamVR tracking

  into one professional XR platform.

  The XR-3 is especially known for:
  - very sharp visuals
  - realistic mixed reality
  - accurate tracking
  - enterprise simulation workflows

  Varjo Bionic Display system

- Field of view:
  Approximately 115°

- Refresh rate:
  90 Hz

  200 Hz integrated eye tracking

- Passthrough:

- Tracking:
  SteamVR Tracking 1.0 / 2.0

- Hand tracking:
  Ultraleap hand tracking

- Connectivity:
  - 2 × DisplayPort
  - 2 × USB 3.0

- PC requirement:
  High-end NVIDIA GPU recommended

- Weight:
  Approximately 594 g

# 7. STEP-BY-STEP First Setup

---

## STEP 1 — Prepare PC

Before setup:
- update GPU driver
- confirm DisplayPort availability
- confirm USB 3.0 ports
- restart computer

---

## STEP 2 — Install Varjo Base

1. Download Varjo Base
2. Install software
3. Restart PC
4. Open Varjo Base

---

## STEP 3 — Connect Headset

Connect:

```text
DisplayPort → PC
USB → PC
Power → Adapter
Headset → Adapter
```

Then open Varjo Base and verify:
```text
Headset detected
```

---

## STEP 4 — Set Up SteamVR Tracking

1. Mount base stations
2. Place them in opposite corners
3. Angle downward
4. Power them on
5. Open SteamVR

Check:
- headset tracking
- controller tracking
- base station status

---

## STEP 5 — Run Room Setup

Inside SteamVR:
1. Set floor height
2. Define play area
3. Confirm boundaries

---

## STEP 6 — Wear Headset

1. Loosen rear strap
2. Put headset on
3. Tighten gently
4. Adjust for clear image

Good fit is important for:
- comfort
- eye tracking
- image clarity

---

## STEP 7 — Eye Tracking Calibration

Inside Varjo Base:
1. Start calibration
2. Follow dots with eyes
3. Finish calibration

This improves:
- gaze tracking
- foveated rendering
- research accuracy

# 8. Understanding Mixed Reality

The XR-3 uses:
```text
camera passthrough
+
virtual objects
```

to create mixed reality.

Workflow:

```text
Real world cameras
↓
Video passthrough
↓
Virtual objects added
↓
Mixed reality view
```

The headset also uses LiDAR for:
- depth sensing
- object placement
- occlusion

# 16. Common Beginner Mistakes

---

## Weak GPU

The XR-3 requires strong graphics hardware.

Weak GPUs cause:
- lag
- stutter
- low FPS

---

## Bad Base Station Placement

Poor placement causes:
- tracking loss
- jitter

---

## Skipping Eye Calibration

This reduces:
- gaze accuracy
- tracking quality

---

## Poor Headset Fit

Causes:
- blurry image
- discomfort
- poor eye tracking

---

## Cable Problems

The XR-3 uses multiple cables.

Always check:
- DisplayPort
- USB
- power adapter

---

# 17. Cleaning & Maintenance

Recommended:
- clean lenses with microfiber cloth
- wipe face cushion after use
- avoid pulling cables
- store headset carefully

Do not:
- spray liquid directly on lenses
- twist cables sharply
