# Artinis PortaLite MKII fNIRS

---

# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  Artinis PortaLite MKII

- Full name:
  Artinis PortaLite MKII fNIRS System

- Device type:
  Portable fNIRS Device / Brain & Muscle Oxygenation Monitor / Functional Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (fNIRS) System

- Category:
  Neurophysiology & Brain Imaging

- Manufacturer:
  Artinis Medical Systems

- Overview:
  The Artinis PortaLite MKII is a portable, wearable:

  ```text
  fNIRS
  (functional Near-Infrared Spectroscopy)
  ```

  system designed for:

  - brain oxygenation monitoring
  - muscle oxygenation monitoring
  - cognitive workload studies
  - neuroergonomics
  - exercise physiology
  - brain function research
  - rehabilitation studies
  - human performance monitoring
  - real-world neuroscience

  Unlike:

  ```text
  EEG
  ```

  which measures:

  ```text
  electrical brain activity
  ```

  the PortaLite MKII measures:

  ```text
  BLOOD OXYGENATION
  INSIDE TISSUE
  ```

  using:

  ```text
  near-infrared light
  ```

  to estimate changes in:

  ```text
  oxygenated hemoglobin (HbO)
  deoxygenated hemoglobin (HbR)
  total hemoglobin (HbT)
  ```

  Think of it as:

  ```text
  LIGHT
  ↓
  BLOOD OXYGENATION CHANGE
  ↓
  BRAIN / MUSCLE ACTIVITY
  ```

  rather than direct electrical signals. It is fully wearable and portable, allowing measurement during movement and outside traditional labs. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

# 4. Hardware Information

## Measurement Principle

The PortaLite MKII uses:

```text
Continuous Wave fNIRS
```

using:

```text
near-infrared light
```

to measure:

```text
hemodynamic change
```

inside tissue.

The system emits light at:

```text
760 nm
850 nm
```

These wavelengths allow measurement of:

```text
oxyhemoglobin
deoxyhemoglobin
```.

---

## Channels

The PortaLite MKII includes:

```text
6 relative channels
+
1 absolute channel
```

Measures:

```text
relative hemoglobin changes
```

plus:

```text
Tissue Saturation Index (TSI)
```

Short separation channels:

```text
built-in
```

to reduce:

```text
superficial scalp blood flow noise
```. 

---

## Sampling Rate

Sampling rate options:

```text
25 Hz
50 Hz
75 Hz
100 Hz
```

This is strong enough for:

```text
hemodynamic response
cognitive tasks
exercise physiology
movement studies
```. 

---

## Sensor Distances

Inter-optode distances:

```text
29 mm
35 mm
41 mm
```

Why this matters:

```text
different distances

different tissue depth
```

Shorter:

```text
surface tissue
```

Longer:

```text
deeper cortex
```.

# 6. What Does It Measure?

---

## Oxygenated Hemoglobin (HbO)

Measures:

```text
oxygen-rich blood
```

Typically:

```text
brain activation
↑
HbO increases
```

Useful for:

- cognition studies
- task difficulty
- workload
- motor activity

---

## Deoxygenated Hemoglobin (HbR)

Measures:

```text
oxygen-depleted blood
```

Useful for:

```text
hemodynamic response
```

Often analyzed with:

```text
HbO
```

together.

---

## Total Hemoglobin (HbT)

Measures:

```text
overall blood volume
```

Useful for:

```text
vascular changes
```

---

## Tissue Saturation Index (TSI)

Very important feature.

Measures:

```text
local oxygen saturation
```

This is:

```text
absolute oxygenation
```

instead of only:

```text
relative change
```. 

---

## Brain OR Muscle

The PortaLite can measure:

### Brain

Examples:

```text
prefrontal cortex
motor cortex
occipital region
```

### Muscle

Examples:

```text
quadriceps
calf
biceps
```

Very useful during:

```text
exercise
fatigue studies
performance testing
```.

# 8. IMPORTANT: Sensor Placement Matters

This matters A LOT.

Good placement:

```text
STABLE CONTACT
```

Bad placement causes:

```text
noise
motion artifact
weak signal
```

For brain recording:

Common location:

```text
forehead
(prefrontal cortex)
```

Hair-heavy regions:

```text
harder to measure
```

because:

```text
hair blocks light
```.
