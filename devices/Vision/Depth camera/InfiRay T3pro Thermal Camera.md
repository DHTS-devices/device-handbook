# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  InfiRay T3 Pro

- Device type:
  Smartphone Thermal Imaging Camera / USB-C Thermal Camera

- Category:
  Vision

- Manufacturer:
  InfiRay / Xinfrared

- Overview:
  The InfiRay T3 Pro is a compact thermal imaging camera designed to connect directly to Android smartphones and tablets through a USB-C interface.

  Unlike standalone thermal cameras such as the FLIR C5, the T3 Pro relies on the smartphone for:
  - display
  - storage
  - app interface
  - image export
  - processing

  The camera itself contains:
  - thermal sensor
  - infrared lens
  - temperature measurement system
  - USB-C interface

  The T3 Pro is designed more as a high-resolution portable thermal imager than a general-purpose consumer gadget. Compared to many lower-end phone thermal cameras, the T3 Pro has:
  - higher thermal resolution
  - manual focus lens
  - long focal length
  - higher measurement range
  - faster frame rate

  The device is commonly used for:
  - electrical inspection
  - PCB diagnostics
  - HVAC inspection
  - home heat leak analysis
  - outdoor observation
  - thermal troubleshooting
  - electronics overheating analysis
  - machine inspection
  - thermal observation at longer distances

  One of the major differences between the T3 Pro and simpler smartphone thermal cameras is the 13 mm lens and manual focusing system, which allows clearer imaging at medium and longer distances. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

- Hardware notes:
  The T3 Pro does NOT contain:
  - built-in display
  - internal battery
  - onboard storage

  This is one of the biggest differences compared to simple fixed-focus thermal phone cameras.

# 5. Main Features

- High-resolution thermal imaging

- 25Hz real-time imaging
- Temperature measurement
- Point / line / area measurement
- Adjustable thermal palettes
- Thermal photo capture
- Thermal video recording
- USB-C smartphone workflow

- Feature notes:
  The biggest strength of the T3 Pro is image clarity compared to many small thermal phone cameras.

  The 13 mm lens allows:
  - clearer medium-distance imaging
  - better target separation
  - improved outdoor observation
  - better PCB inspection detail

  compared to ultra-wide short focal thermal cameras. :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}

- Important practical note:
  Manual focus is extremely important on this camera.

  If focus is incorrect:
  - thermal edges become blurry
  - temperature readings become less reliable
  - small hotspots disappear
  - PCB details become difficult to identify

  Beginners often forget to refocus when changing target distance.

---

# 7.3 Manual Focus Workflow

This is one of the most important parts of using the T3 Pro properly.

Steps:
1. Point camera at target
2. Rotate focus ring slowly
3. Watch thermal edges carefully
4. Stop when edges become sharp
5. Capture image

- Important:
  Focus distance changes depending on:
  - target distance
  - target size
  - thermal contrast

  Every time the user changes distance significantly, refocusing is usually necessary.

# 7.4 Electronics / PCB Inspection Workflow

Common workflow:
1. Power on device under test
2. Open thermal app
3. Focus carefully
4. Scan PCB slowly
5. Look for abnormal hotspots
6. Compare neighboring components
7. Capture reference images

Typical things visible:
- overheating ICs
- short circuits
- hot regulators
- VRM heating
- GPU hotspots
- power rail imbalance

- Important practical note:
  Shiny metal heatsinks can reflect infrared energy and produce misleading readings.

# 11. Troubleshooting

## Camera Not Detected

Possible causes:
- OTG disabled
- unsupported phone
- app permissions missing
- USB-C connection issue

Possible solutions:
- reconnect device
- restart app
- enable OTG
- reboot phone

---

## Blurry Thermal Image

Possible causes:
- focus incorrect
- lens dirty
- moving too quickly

Possible solutions:
- refocus manually
- clean lens carefully
- stabilize phone

- Notes:
  Manual focus is critical on the T3 Pro.

---

## Temperature Reading Seems Incorrect

Possible causes:
- reflective surface
- emissivity issue
- wrong focus
- environmental reflection

Possible solutions:
- change angle
- compare nearby surfaces
- improve focus
- avoid shiny metal surfaces

---

## Excessive Phone Heating

Possible causes:
- long recording sessions
- high processing load
- thermal video recording

Possible solutions:
- reduce session length
- cool phone
- close background apps

---

## App Crashes or Freezes

Possible causes:
- incompatible Android version
- insufficient memory
- unstable USB connection

Possible solutions:
- restart app
- update app
- reconnect device
- reboot phone

- The InfiRay T3 Pro is designed mainly for:
  - portable thermal diagnostics
  - detailed thermal inspection
  - smartphone thermal workflows

  rather than scientific laboratory thermography.

- Compared to many entry-level phone thermal cameras:
  - higher resolution
  - sharper imaging
  - manual focus
  - longer focal length
  - better medium-distance performance

- Compared to standalone thermal cameras:
  - more portable
  - smartphone-dependent
  - lower power consumption

- Common beginner mistakes:
  - forgetting to refocus manually
  - misunderstanding reflective surfaces
  - assuming thermal cameras see through walls
  - moving camera too quickly
  - trusting one temperature reading without comparison

- Practical advice:
  The T3 Pro works best when:
  - focus is adjusted carefully
  - comparisons are made between nearby surfaces
  - the user understands reflective thermal behavior
  - thermal patterns are interpreted slowly and carefully

- The T3 Pro is especially strong for:
  - PCB inspection
  - electronics troubleshooting
  - electrical maintenance
  - HVAC diagnostics
  - thermal observation at moderate distances
