# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  FLIR C5 Thermal Imaging Camera

- Device type:
  Thermal Imaging Camera / Infrared Camera

- Category:
  Vision

- Manufacturer:
  FLIR Systems / Teledyne FLIR

- Overview:
  The FLIR C5 is a compact handheld thermal imaging camera designed for detecting and visualizing temperature differences across surfaces and environments.

  Unlike a normal camera that captures visible light, the FLIR C5 detects infrared radiation (heat energy) emitted by objects and converts that information into a thermal image. Warmer areas appear differently from cooler areas depending on the selected thermal color palette.

  The camera is mainly designed for:
  - electrical inspection
  - HVAC inspection
  - water leak detection
  - insulation analysis
  - equipment overheating detection
  - building diagnostics
  - environmental thermal observation

  Because the camera records temperature variation instead of visible color only, it can reveal:
  - overheating electronics
  - hidden water leaks
  - poor insulation
  - air leaks
  - heat signatures
  - thermal imbalance

  The FLIR C5 combines:
  - thermal camera
  - visible light camera
  - touchscreen interface
  - Wi-Fi connectivity
  - cloud upload support

  into a single portable device.

  The device is especially useful when:
  - temperature differences matter
  - invisible heat patterns need to be visualized
  - inspection needs to be portable and fast

  The C5 is designed as a field inspection tool rather than a laboratory-grade scientific thermal camera. ([flir.com](https://www.flir.com/products/c5/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

# 5. Main Features

- Thermal imaging
- Temperature spot measurement
- MSX image enhancement
- Touchscreen interface
- Wi-Fi connectivity
- Cloud upload support
- Picture-in-picture overlay
- Portable handheld operation
- Thermal photo capture
- Thermal inspection workflows

- Feature notes:
  The most important feature for most users is the ability to quickly identify temperature differences visually.

  Instead of reading numbers only, the camera allows the user to immediately see:
  - hot spots
  - cold spots
  - uneven heating
  - hidden thermal problems

  Common examples:
  - overheating breaker panel
  - warm electrical outlet
  - missing insulation
  - water leak inside wall
  - overheating laptop charger
  - hot motor bearing

- Important practical note:
  Thermal cameras do NOT see through walls.

  This is one of the most common misunderstandings.

  The camera only measures surface temperature differences.

  For example:
  - a water leak may cool the wall surface
  - insulation problems may warm or cool the surface
  - overheated wires may warm the outer panel

  but the camera is not directly seeing inside the object.

# 7.4 Understanding Thermal Colors

Thermal palettes are visual representations of temperature differences.

Examples:
- white-hot
- black-hot
- rainbow
- iron
- lava

Important:
- colors do NOT represent actual object color
- colors only represent relative temperature

A blue object in thermal view may not actually be blue physically.

---

# 7.5 Understanding Reflections

Thermal cameras can be affected by infrared reflections.

Common reflective surfaces:
- metal
- glass
- polished surfaces
- shiny electronics

Example:
A shiny metal surface may reflect:
- your body heat
- sunlight
- nearby heat source

instead of showing the true surface temperature.

This is one of the biggest beginner mistakes in thermal imaging interpretation.

# 7.7 Outdoor Usage Notes

- sunlight
- wind
- rain
- surface heating
- time of day

Best practices:
- avoid direct midday sun when possible
- compare shaded surfaces
- avoid rain exposure
- allow camera to acclimate to temperature

# 11. Troubleshooting

## Thermal Image Looks Incorrect

Possible causes:
- reflective surface
- incorrect emissivity
- environmental reflections
- temperature stabilization issue

Possible solutions:
- change viewing angle
- avoid reflective materials
- allow camera to stabilize
- compare nearby surfaces

---

## Blurry Thermal Image

Possible causes:
- target too close
- moving camera too quickly
- lens contamination

Possible solutions:
- move farther away
- hold camera steady
- clean lens carefully

---

## Temperature Reading Seems Wrong

Possible causes:
- reflective materials
- emissivity mismatch
- sunlight interference

Possible solutions:
- avoid shiny surfaces
- compare multiple angles
- reduce environmental interference

---

## Wi-Fi Upload Problems

Possible causes:
- weak signal
- account issue
- network restriction

Possible solutions:
- reconnect Wi-Fi
- restart device
- upload manually through USB

---

## Device Battery Drains Quickly

Possible causes:
- screen brightness
- Wi-Fi usage
- long continuous operation

Possible solutions:
- reduce brightness
- disable Wi-Fi
- recharge fully before field use

# 12. Notes

- The FLIR C5 is designed primarily as a portable inspection and diagnostic tool.

- Compared to standard cameras:
  - detects heat instead of visible light
  - visualizes temperature differences
  - useful for hidden thermal problems

- Compared to higher-end industrial thermal cameras:
  - lower thermal resolution
  - simpler workflow
  - more portable
  - easier for general inspection use

- Common beginner mistakes:
  - assuming thermal cameras see through walls
  - ignoring reflective surfaces
  - misunderstanding thermal palettes
  - trusting single measurements without comparison

- Practical advice:
  Thermal imaging works best when comparing temperature differences rather than focusing only on absolute temperature numbers.

- Good thermal imaging practice usually involves:
  - comparing nearby surfaces
  - checking multiple angles
  - understanding environment conditions
  - documenting context with notes and visible images
