# Vscan Air Ultrasound

---

# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  GE HealthCare Vscan Air

- Device type:
  Wireless Handheld Ultrasound System / Point-of-Care Ultrasound (POCUS)

- Category:
  Medical Imaging

- Manufacturer:
  [GE HealthCare](https://www.gehealthcare.com?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

- Overview:
  The Vscan Air is a wireless handheld ultrasound system designed for:
  - bedside imaging
  - rapid assessment
  - emergency medicine
  - cardiac scanning
  - vascular imaging
  - musculoskeletal imaging
  - abdominal scanning
  - point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS)

  Unlike traditional cart-based ultrasound systems, the Vscan Air is:
  - pocket-sized
  - wireless
  - app-based
  - battery powered

  and connects directly to:
  - smartphones
  - tablets

  using Wi-Fi and Bluetooth. 

  One of the biggest reasons the Vscan Air became popular is because it combines:
  - dual probes
  - portability
  - wireless workflow
  - whole-body scanning capability

  into a single handheld device. 

  The Vscan Air exists mainly in two major versions:

| Model | Probe Combination |
|---|---|
| Vscan Air CL | Curved + Linear |
| Vscan Air SL | Sector + Linear |

  The CL model is usually used for:
  - abdominal
  - OB/GYN
  - general scanning

  while the SL model is optimized more for:
  - cardiac
  - vascular
  - lung imaging.

```text

THE ULTRASOUND SCREEN
```

2. Verify charging indicator

```text
~75 minutes
```

# STEP 2 — Install The App

```text
Vscan Air App
```

1. Select probe type:

---

# 9. Understanding Ultrasound Gel

The gel is REQUIRED.

Without gel:
```text
AIR BLOCKS ULTRASOUND
```

Gel removes air between:
- probe
- skin

allowing sound transmission.

---

Without gel:
```text
image quality becomes terrible
```

## Mistake 2 — Pressing Too Hard

Too much pressure:
- distorts tissue
- compresses vessels

---

## Mistake 3 — Wrong Probe Choice

Linear:
```text
shallow
```

Curved/sector:
```text
deep
```

---

## Mistake 4 — Moving Too Fast

Ultrasound requires:
```text
slow controlled movement
```

---

## Mistake 5 — Incorrect Angle

Tiny angle changes dramatically affect:
- image quality
- visibility
