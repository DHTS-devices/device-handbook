# 1. Overview

- Device name:
  FLIR ONE Pro Thermal Imaging Camera for Android USB-C

- Device type:
  Smartphone Thermal Imaging Camera / Mobile Infrared Camera

- Category:
  Vision

- Manufacturer:
  Teledyne FLIR

- Overview:
  The FLIR ONE Pro is a compact thermal imaging camera designed to attach directly to an Android smartphone through a USB-C connector.

  Unlike standalone thermal cameras such as the FLIR C5, the FLIR ONE Pro relies on the smartphone for:
  - display
  - storage
  - processing
  - sharing
  - app control

  The camera itself contains:
  - thermal sensor
  - visible light camera
  - internal battery
  - USB-C connector

  The device allows users to visualize heat differences and temperature patterns directly from a phone screen.

  It is commonly used for:
  - electrical troubleshooting
  - HVAC inspection
  - leak detection
  - electronics overheating checks
  - PC thermal analysis
  - automotive diagnostics
  - appliance inspection
  - quick field diagnostics

  The biggest advantage of the FLIR ONE Pro is portability. It can fit into a pocket and quickly attach to a phone when needed. ([flir.com](https://www.flir.com/products/flir-one-pro/) )

  Unlike normal phone cameras, the FLIR ONE Pro detects infrared radiation instead of visible light. This allows users to identify:
  - hot spots
  - cold spots
  - uneven heating
  - overheating circuits
  - airflow leaks
  - insulation problems

  The camera uses FLIR MSX technology, which overlays visible image edges onto the thermal image to improve readability. ([flir.com](https://www.flir.com/products/flir-one-pro/) )

- Smartphone integration
- Thermal photo capture
- Thermal video recording
- Multiple thermal palettes
- Spot temperature measurement
- Hot and cold tracking
- Portable pocket-sized design

- Feature notes:
  The FLIR ONE Pro is designed more as a practical troubleshooting tool than a scientific thermal measurement instrument.

  The camera is especially useful because users can immediately:
  - capture images
  - annotate problems
  - send reports
  - share findings
  - upload images

  directly from the phone.

- Important practical note:
  Thermal cameras do NOT see through walls.

  This is one of the most common misunderstandings.

  The camera only detects surface heat differences.

  Example:
  - a leaking pipe may cool the wall surface
  - overloaded wiring may heat the panel surface

  but the camera is not literally seeing inside the wall.

# 7.5 Understanding Thermal Colors

Thermal palettes include:
- Iron
- Rainbow
- Arctic
- Lava
- White hot
- Black hot

Important:
- colors represent temperature differences
- colors are NOT the object's actual color

A blue area in thermal mode does not mean the object is physically blue.

---

# 7.6 Reflective Surface Problems

Thermal cameras are strongly affected by reflective materials.

Problem surfaces:
- polished metal
- glass
- glossy surfaces
- mirrors

Example:
A shiny metal pipe may reflect:
- your body heat
- sunlight
- nearby heat source

instead of showing its true temperature.

This is one of the biggest beginner mistakes.

# 11. Troubleshooting

## Camera Not Detected by Phone

Possible causes:
- App permissions missing
- Loose USB-C connection
- Unsupported Android device
- Low battery

Possible solutions:
- Restart app
- Reconnect camera
- Charge camera
- Remove thick phone case
- Restart phone

---

## Thermal Image Looks Incorrect

Possible causes:
- reflective surface
- sunlight interference
- thermal stabilization issue

Possible solutions:
- change angle
- avoid reflective materials
- wait for stabilization

---

## Battery Drains Quickly

Possible causes:
- long imaging sessions
- screen brightness
- video recording

Possible solutions:
- reduce screen brightness
- recharge camera
- use power bank

---

## Temperature Reading Seems Wrong

Possible causes:
- shiny surface
- wrong thermal range
- sunlight reflection

Possible solutions:
- compare nearby surfaces
- adjust measurement range
- avoid reflective objects

---

## App Freezing or Disconnecting

Possible causes:
- Android USB issue
- app crash
- overheating

Possible solutions:
- restart app
- reconnect camera
- reboot phone
